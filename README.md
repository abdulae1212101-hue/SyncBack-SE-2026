## 🛡️ Best Practices for Data Security

Protecting your information requires discipline. Follow these tips to keep your data secure.

- Test your backups. Restore a file once a month to ensure it works. 
- Keep your external drive plugged in. The software cannot save files if it cannot find the destination.
- Use a dedicated drive. Do not mix your backup folders with your regular files.
- Store backups in different places. Keep one copy on a local hard drive and another on a cloud server if possible. 
- Check the history log. Look at the logs once a week to see if any errors occurred during the transfer.

The software pauses the process. It resumes the backup the next time you turn on your computer and open the program.

Can I move my backup folder to a new computer?
Yes. You can plug your external drive into any Windows machine. You can then copy or open the files directly.

Is my data private?
Yes. You control where the files go. The software does not send your data to a third-party server.

Does the software slow my computer down?
The impact is minimal. You can set the backup to run at night while you sleep to avoid any interruptions.

Why did my backup fail?
Check the log file. It lists reasons like a missing drive or a read-only setting.

Can I sync two different computers?
Yes. You can use the sync feature to make folders on two machines identical.
